Croquetas

Description

Croquetas are a popular Spanish snack made from a creamy mixture of béchamel sauce and various fillings such as ham, chicken, or cheese. These bite-sized delights are coated in breadcrumbs and fried to golden perfection, creating a crispy exterior that contrasts with the soft, savory filling inside. Known for their rich and comforting flavors, croquetas are often served as tapas or appetizers.

A favorite in Spanish cuisine, croquetas offer versatility, allowing for different fillings depending on regional traditions or personal preference. Whether enjoyed at family gatherings or in restaurants, these flavorful bites are a beloved dish that pairs well with a variety of sauces and dips, making them an essential part of any Spanish meal.

ingredients

  • Butter
  • Flour
  • Milk
  • Nutmeg
  • Cured ham
  • Eggs
  • Oregano
  • Salt and pepper
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Heat the melted butter in a small saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Add the flour and cook for 3 minutes, stirring constantly. Turn the heat to the lowest setting and very slowly add the milk, stirring constantly to avoid creating lumps.
  3. Heat the melted butter in a small saucepan over medium heat.
  4. Add the flour and cook for 3 minutes, stirring constantly.
  5. Turn the heat to the lowest setting and very slowly add the milk, stirring constantly to avoid creating lumps.
  6. Add the minced ham and continue to cook for about 2 to 3 minutes, continuing to stir.
  7. Remove from heat; taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
  8. Allow to cool for 5 to 10 minutes and then refrigerate at least for 3 hours until the mixture is cold.
  9. Beat the eggs in a bowl; pour the breadcrumbs into another small, wide bowl.
  10. Preheat the vegetable oil into a large and deep frying pan until it reaches 350°F/180°C.
  11. Cover your hands in flour, then divide the mixture into 1-inch balls.
  12. Dip the croquettes in the beaten egg and coat with breadcrumbs by rolling in the bowl.
  13. Carefully place the croquettes in the hot oil and fry quickly, in batches, turning several times, until golden and all the croquettes are fried.
  14. Remove the croquettes with a slotted spoon and set on a paper towel to absorb the excess oil.
  15. Serve immediately.
